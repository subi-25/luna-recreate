Our Team

Gloria Mendez | LPC-S, CGP, CCTP-II, CNLP Owner/Director of Clinical Operations/Therapist

As the Owner and Director of Luna Evolution Counseling, Gloria brings a holistic, client-centered approach to therapy that honors each individuals unique path toward growth and healing. With a strong foundation in trauma-informed care, Gloria specializes in helping adults navigate anxiety, life transitions, self-esteem challenges, and emotional regulation.
Grounded in the belief that healing is a journey of evolution, Gloria integrates evidence-based practices such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), mindfulness, and dialectical-behavior therapy (DBT) to support lasting change. With 27+ years of experience in both clinical and community-based settings, she is known for creating a warm, inclusive, and nonjudgmental space where clients feel truly seen and supported.
Through Luna Evolution Counseling, Gloria is dedicated to helping clients reconnect with their inner strength, rediscover clarity, and evolve toward their most authentic selves.

Annalyne Vasquez | LPC-Associate Supervised by Michelle Holcombs, LPC-S

Hi, Im Annie Vasquez, a Licensed Professional Counselor Associate with a passion for providing client-centered care and offering unconditional positive regard in every therapeutic relationship. I specialize in working with adults ages 18 to 40 who are navigating challenges related to anxiety, depression, grief, and ADHD. My therapeutic approach is primarily behavioral, integrating Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) techniques, while also incorporating holistic and mindfulness-based strategies to support overall well-being.
I feel honored to walk alongside my clients as they navigate lifes challenges and work toward finding a sense of peace and fulfillment.

Cruz Fox | LPC-Associate Supervised by Gloria Mendez, LPC-S

Meet Cruz Fox, a Mexican American man and Licensed Professional Counselor- Associate (He/Him). Cruz graduated from UTSA in 2020 with his Bachelor of Arts in Psychology and again in 2024 with his Master of Science in Clinical Mental Health Counseling. He pulls from relational cultural theory, person-centered therapy, and narrative therapy to help guide his practice. He uses empathy to help clients explore meaning, emotion, connection, and culture. Cruz is dedicated to creating a safe and non-judgmental counseling space, promoting vulnerability and honesty. He is passionate about social advocacy and understanding how people navigate and change systems. Cruz works with individuals aged 10 and up, struggling with anxiety, ADHD, borderline, depression, grief, trauma, self-esteem, relationship issues, adjustment, and LGBTQ+ issues. Cruz offers couples counseling and has availability Monday through Friday.
